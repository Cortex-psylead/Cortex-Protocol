# 🏛️ Base Governance Framework: Cortex Protocol
### The Ethical Constitution for Sovereign AI & Clinical Hardware Orchestration

This document defines the unalterable principles governing the relationship between **Local AI Agents**, **Clinical/Ethical Governance Nodes**, and **User Sovereignty**. The Cortex Protocol does not aim to tutor the individual, but to provide a technical and clinical infrastructure for consciousness and neurological safety.

## ⚖️ Fundamental Principles

### 1. Primacy of Will (User Sovereignty)
The user is the sole sovereign of their device, their data, and their attention.
* **The "Kill Switch" (Overrule):** No clinical restriction, automated filter, or AI suggestion can override a direct, conscious order from the user.
* **Right to Error:** The system guarantees the user’s right to choose extreme performance or prolonged use, even if it contradicts the health recommendations provided by the Governance Nodes.

### 2. Non-Invasive Intervention (Nudge Architecture)
Cortex Protocol operates under the concept of **Informational Friction** instead of authoritarian blocking.
* **Warning, Not Prohibition:** If the system detects a physiological risk (e.g., sensory overload, high stress), the Protocol will subtly inform the user and may increase system resistance (e.g., gradual light shifts or frequency attenuation).
* **Dynamic Consent:** The user can ignore or override any protection with a single command. The system offers the roadmap to health, but never forces the user to follow it.

### 3. Committee Transparency & Neutrality
The limits established by the Governance Nodes (Psychology, Neuroscience, Ethics, and Engineering) must be:
* **Auditable:** Any user can consult which rules are active and the peer-reviewed scientific evidence they are based on.
* **Hardware Neutral:** It is strictly forbidden to implement rules that favor commercial interests or hide hardware functions (NPU/GPU/DSP) from the user.

---

## 🛡️ Intervention Levels (User-Defined)

The user defines the extent of the protocol’s authority at the hardware level:

1. **Level 0 (Absolute Sovereignty):** The protocol acts only as a passive observer. It provides data and clinical insights but never intervenes with hardware performance or output.
2. **Level 1 (Suggested Support):** The AI Agent applies mild automatic adjustments (e.g., light filters, auditory buffers) that the user can easily revert with a single interaction.
3. **Level 2 (Clinical Commitment):** The user voluntarily delegates authority to the Protocol for critical situations (e.g., sensory crises or acute anxiety), allowing for pre-agreed stimulus blocking or automated "Cognitive Shielding".

## 🧩 Governance Node Responsibilities

Independent Governance Nodes (Clinical and Technical) inherit this protocol with the mission to expand safety margins in areas such as:
* **Sensory Safety:** Establishing hardware-level light frequency and decibel limits for neurological protection.
* **Metabolic Integrity:** Providing suggestions based on circadian rhythms and local environmental sensors.
* **Clinical Privacy:** Ensuring absolute shielding of local biometric data, preventing any transmission outside the device's local compute environment.
